It's now been a week since I arrived and I've all ready done so much.  Just friday I watched a man kill two rattle snakes and two taiwanese poisonous somethings.  The rattlesnake is all ready pretty well known, but the Taiwanese snake, according to the men there, can kill a person in half an hour.   Both of these snakes are considered rare and are protected either locally or globally.  A few moments later I drank the rice wine blood mixture that had a bit of an organ that when described to me sounded like the spleen.
Though this to me was a high point the rest of the week was full of adventure as well. I tried for the first time something called a Betelnut. A seed that is wrapped in a leaf and chewed like tobacco.  When it came time to spit the fluids out they came out as a blood red color they made it look like I had internal bleeding.  After chewing for only a few minutes I felt almost drunk.
The dragonboat festival happened on Wednesday and though it's labeled as the second most celebrated event in Taiwan and China it cannot hold a candle to Chinese New Year.  People come to race in boats shaped like, you guessed it, a dragon.  The festival we went to was held in a harbor, people from all around the world came to compete.  There were even some Americans that came and tried to fight for the title.  It seemed like all the western teams got paired together to help eliminate them early, though it probably just seemed that way.  Like any celebration, this one had lots of food to eat.  Some traditional stuff to eat and some just really good food.  Kites lined the sky for the kite flying competition that happens the same day.
We came by a lake that once a year would hold an event where anyone willing enough was to swim across it.  It was in that same day that I saw how beautiful all of Taiwan really is.  The mountains are dramatic and abundant.  Each one is covered with trees and other vegetation.  It's an incredible sight to behold.
My experience learning the language thus far has been difficult.  It didn't realize how bad my language skills were until I found that I was grasping for words to form even the most simple of sentences.  But the most difficult part is learning the tones.  To help remind me how bad my tones are, I have the resident two year old making fun of me.
Speaking of which, just now we stopped off at a Gas Station to grab a few snacks for the road on our way east.  I tell you that Yuyin is a tricky girl.  We got some food that she isn't allowed to have cause it's spicy, something called pea crackers.   She kept asking for it and asking for it.  Finally, we got into the car and I was given them because I was in the front seat.  She asked me to give it to her, I of course told her I wouldn't give it to her.  She said, it's not for me, I'm going to give it to my mother.  She's a trickster she is.  I'm on to her game.
This has been written rather frantically, so it's probably pretty jumbled and terribly written.  All I can say is this, the past week has been exciting and confusing all at the same time.  I'm starting to understand the language more and more, but my speaking ability is just bad. I've seen and experienced more in one week then most do in one year.  That's probably an exaggeration, but I have seen a lot.  Likewise, I know I'll see more, but starting this week I have to hunker down and do what I came to do.  Hopefully my grasp of the language will increase over the next month or so.  We shall see.
