--- layout: post title: Oily date: 2010-05-06 16:09:46.000000000 -04:00 categories: - Life tags: [] status: publish type: post published: true --- Just from the news perspective, these past two weeks have been crazy. That massive oil spill that seems to threaten all of the gulf coast. It seems increasingly imperative to keep this off of the shore. The entire thing has the potential to destroy the fishing market in the gulf coast, if it hasn't all ready. One amazing thing I heard and I didn't realize this, but apparently the Exxon spill off of Alaska has left a lasting impact on the shore where its been over 20 years and still oil can be found all over the shore and fish in the area are still being caught with oil in their bellies. That's terrifying. I've always been a strong proponent of an oil free economy, at least in the realm of consumer automobiles among other things. I've never had the funds to support such a thing, but I have actively pursued gas and oil free alternatives when I have had the chance. I rarely if ever go on campus with my car and when I can I ride the train. I personally wish that the price of oil would skyrocket making it economically infeasible to use as a fuel source and thus in this free market society the market would work to create an alternative. Right now, there is little incentive. Many would like to see more alternatives on the market, but the cost involved ultimately finds that it is cheaper just to go with an old car that consumes slightly more gas. Some of course don't believe in global warming and thus don't care if petroleum based products are used. Even if there is doubt to the possibility of global warming the thought of maintaining oil as central to our way of life is fundamentally flawed in too many ways to count. First, the most obvious thing we've seen from this recent spill is that oil is dirty. Quite frankly it's dirty as hell. It's hard to clean up and has profound affects on the wildlife around that would come in contact with it. Even if we could take every possible precaution with flawless precision, accidents still happen. When they do, the outcome is disastrous. The oil will eventual settle and the wildlife and environment will go back to normal, but the time that it takes will be well beyond our lifetime. Second, it's a limited resource. There are debates as to how soon we will run out, but what is abundantly clear is that a fossil fuel is very finite and if we don't run out in the next ten years or fifty years we will run out within a foreseeable future. Lastly, it's costly. But this works into the how it is dirty and nonrenewable. Oil has to be found transported, refined, stored, transported and ultimately it can get to your car. I don't actually know if the process is more complicated than that, but it is at least that complicated. Unfortunately, there aren't really any viable power supplies that can create a similar amount of power without having the same steps. Though there is the possibility of getting an electric car that runs off a grid that uses only wind power, that'd be sweet. Of course there are mountain of problem associated with using a solely electric based car, mainly that goes into poor power storage technology. That's a totally different issue. I cannot fathom why everyone is so bent on maintaining this filthy, expensive and limited resource. My feeling is that so long as it is cheap and relatively easy to get we will never get off of our dependency of oil. The only hope we have is to have technology that effectively makes it the more costly option, even in the short term. I hope that day comes soon. @Tab
