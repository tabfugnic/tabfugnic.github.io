Black Friday is that day where everything is so criminally discounted that people have been known to draw blood to take advantage of the savings. Last year people were even killed from being trampled on. Where the day before people have fallen into a tryptophan induced coma by four o'clock so they can wake up at the wee hours of the night to wait in line in an often very cold and sometimes, like today, very wet setting. To some people though it's well worth the savings.

Personally I don't understand Black Friday. I don't find it even remotely appealing to wait in line before the crack of dawn, even if it's for incredible savings. Granted right now I don't find it even remotely appealing to spend any sort of money. It's sort of hypocritical of me to say anything negative about those people that wait in line so early to get stuff on Black Friday. I've never been there for Black Friday, but one time I waited in line for a Wii. At least I was hanging out with people I knew, so it was fun, but still stupid.

What is good about Black Friday is that it signifies the official start of the Christmas Season. Though many stores have all ready started putting out Christmas merchandise as far back as September. Christmas is an exciting time of year, but it's importance to me is inline quite similarly with Thanksgiving. My favorite part about Christmas and Thanksgiving aren't about the presents or the food. It's always about seeing my family and being able to interact with them. All the other stuff are just perks.

Happy Thanksgiving to Everyone.
