So I'm staying in this shady place called the ChungKing Mansions. How shady is it, you ask? Well, let me describe it. As I get out of the metro station I look up to see this beat up building with signs that clearly haven't lit up this decade or even the past decade for that matter. Before I can walk in the main gate I have two men separately trying to convince me that I need to stay in their hostel, and whatever hostel I've booked is far worse. They even say, "just look at it, I give you that for free, how many people would give you something for free here." Of course it doesn't end there. After I walk in the front door, I have two more men telling me I must stay in their hostel. One finally tells me the direction I need to go to get to my hostel, which happens to be a guarded elevator that fits maybe five thin people, but it claims it can fit eight. As I wait in line to get in a man steps out of the one I'm in front of, looks at me really quick and says, "You need a guesthouse?" Mind you I've had one or two more asking me to stay at their place, between that time. Total time till I enter the elevator, about 15 minutes.

I finally come to the hostel I booked get everything squared away and come to my room. A small room with a bed that can barely fit my long body when I sit up and a bathroom that you could barely sit down in. When I mean sit down, I mean on the toilet, you could not sit down on the ground. Granted I don't think I really want to. The hostel itself lives up to its name of being incredibly clean. I can't much complain, I get free internet and a place to sleep and I'm not spending that much money considering. It would not surprise me if I spend more on one meal than I'd spend one night here. Until then I need to figure out what to do and where to go. I'm starving.
