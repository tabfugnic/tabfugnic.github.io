I'm going to try an experiment. I'm going to use each successive month, until this time next year to learn something new. Something that is large enough that it will take the entire month to learn it. This includes things that are too large for the entire month, but not so large that by the end of it I've hardly scratched in a tiny bit of the surface. I'm looking primarily at creating a sort of working understanding of the subject by the end of the month.

I hope to get a few things out of this. First and most obvious, I will have learned or have started my way on a new skill. Second and third are in my documenting of these adventures. In posting all that I know I will hopefully become more comfortable and better at writing and my documentation will hopefully help other around me. And finally, it will give me something interesting to do and I may even meet some new faces along the way.

I need to set up some rules to go by:

  1. The topic I choose must be large enough to span an entire month.
  2. I must write about something every day, without fail
  3. If I miss one day then I have to write enough the next day to encompass that last one, if I miss that day then I need to write enough to encompass both and so on.
  4. Posting more than once a day will only marginally impact how much I am required to post the next day.
  5. I have to present something new every day in my post, and each post must be over 50 words.
  6. Days start at 6am. So if I post something at 3am on April 2nd, 2011 it counts for the day April 1st, 2011.

The previous rules may be broken in certain situations.  These include, but are not limited to situations that limit computer access and emergencies.

I am allowed to add rules as I see fit.

My first thing I will start with for the month of April is Ruby on Rails.  Tonight I will do a post on the things I will be working on.  This is a bit of a cheat because I have worked with rails before, but this will be a good start.

Let the challenge begin!
