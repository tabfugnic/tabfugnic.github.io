Before I came to Taiwan I looked for places to climb for while I was here. Unfortunately, any available literature was either more than ten years old or just didn't provide nearly enough information. Some would provide links to places that were only in Chinese or just wouldn't exist. So when I first came I used what little knowledge I had to find places to climb. The locals were even less help than the online guides. Most of the locals seem to believe that climbing wasn't something Taiwanese do.

After going over all of my options I finally looked up one from a guide about ten years old on the internet. It was intriguing, a climbing wall setup on the third story of an apartment building started by a climbing club that wanted a place to hang out and climb. At least to me this had sounded like an interesting group of people. The place was only marked by an unlit sign in Chinese at the base of the building. Don't ask me how I ended up actually finding it, but I did. My feeling of accomplishment was turned to uneasiness as I looked up a staircase that only slightly better lit than the sign. I had come this far, and refused to turn back. When getting to the top, I had been pleasantly surprised. A brightly lit climbing area with enthusiastic people doing crazy spiderman climbs all over the place.

The wall itself is very utilitarian and covers a space of about two medium sized living rooms. There are just walls, which encompass nearly the entirety of the space save a bathroom and a minuscule locker room in the back. There is also a tiny space to get chalk and read old Naruto Manga in Chinese. The climbing walls cover not only the walls, but take advantage of the entire ceiling as well. The crowd of people coming here encompass the entire "employee" roster which is somewhere in the range of about fifteen people. The rest of the people that come here are girlfriends of those climbers and random people including the expats. The wall is small and crowded to be sure, but the price and atmosphere are perfect. It costs a mere $50 NTD(approx. $1.50 USD).

It's pretty clear that this wall had been built to accommodate these guys love of climbing and to make a bit of money on the side. These guys are ridiculous, ripped out of their mind and doing some of the most elaborate climbing I've ever seen. I haven't seen this yet, but I've been told that these guys do something called "running ceiling laps." This is where they spiderman back and forth along the ceiling, I sight I can't wait to see.

In the two times that I've climbed there, I've tapered out after only about an hour with my arms feeling like jelly. This is mostly because I'm out of practice, but a lot is because of the unbelievable heat in this confined space that doesn't have air conditioning. Within only the first climb, sweat all ready starts to pour out of me.

Thankfully, like any climbing wall, it's a friendly bunch. There is a language barrier, but they all really nice and helpful people. The few expats their vary drastically, from being really friendly, to really quiet, to just being cold. It's an incredible place to say the least, and I look forward to use it as my training for Long Dong!
