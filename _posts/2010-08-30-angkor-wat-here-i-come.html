There has been so much that has happened since my last post. I finished my work at Acer, drove across Taiwan, and flew to another island off of the island of Taiwan. I could write for a long time about each one of them, because each was unique and wonderful. That's not what I'm going to do.

I just came back from my trip to Penghu. It's that island off the coast of the island of Taiwan. Really it's an archipelago, but it's easier to refer to it as an island. It was relaxing trip. I learned how to surf from an Aussie that's been living on the island. In a crazy twist when visiting another still smaller island, I got pushed into a chinese only tour group and was ushered around in a bus all around the place. I'm sure the tour would've been a lot more interesting if I understood him completely, but I only caught a bit of it.

Now I'm sitting in an airport about to head to Siem Reap, the city that sits right next to Angkor Wat. It should be an interesting adventure to a recently de-landmined city that uses only US currency where I can ride elephants!

I don't actually have much to say, but I know I will be saying more as things come along.
