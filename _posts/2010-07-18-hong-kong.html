I had written a much longer and fully featured post about Hong Kong, but now that that is nearly a week away, I feel it'd be better to do a summary that hit on a few key points.

Hong Kong has several subtle things that make it feel like a European city with weird characters everywhere, but it is something else all its own. It's surprising that such a small area can have in one instance a busy metropolis, then going only forty minutes by train you can find a beautiful untouched landscape. I'm sure there are other places in the world like this, but, honestly, I can't think of any. The one thing that seems to have defined my stay there was the heat. Taiwan is hot, but Hong Kong, at least while I was there, was unbearable. I would walk a block and quite literally my shirt would be soaked and my nether regions were the swampiest of swamps.

During my stay I did a lot of touristy type things, like looking at temples. One temple, called the 10,000 Buddha temple, was just as it says, it had over 10,000 Buddhas. You can see the pictures online, it was quite the site. Other temple visits boasted the world's largest Buddha and wisdom trails.

I did my fair share of wandering around shops, looking at computer stores cause I'm a geek and going through street markets. I spent a whopping $17 USD during my stay on things I needed. The rest of my money went to sleep, food and the best part of all, drink. I don't drink that much in Taiwan, partially because the alcohol sucks, but mostly because I just work a lot and don't feel like going out to drink. Fortunately enough, I had a friend with me that gave me more reason to drink.

The few nights I went out I had some odd things happen to me. The first night I went to a pub and conversed with a Korean man and his son for a few hours only to finally come to the bill and he graciously paid for the whole thing, despite my objections. The next two nights I spent with my friend watching soccer from 2:30 AM til 5:00 AM. The first of those nights we spent it on Hong Kong Island which my hostel was not on and the subway stops running at 1:00AM. The next night we watched from outside a bar cause we didn't want to pay the $200 HKD($30USD). Every bar was doing it so there wasn't any point.

The next few days were a bit of a haze as I faded in and out of sleep. I still saw some cool stuff, went to the top of Victorian Peak on this unforgettably vertical decent up a mountain in a train.

In the midst of all this, I ate plenty of amazing food where most of it wasn't Kong Kong food, but amazing nonetheless.

Hong Kong is a crazy place. It's a lot of fun despite the heat, but I don't think I'd ever be able to go for more than a week.
