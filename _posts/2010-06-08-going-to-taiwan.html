Well, I thought I had written this out in a word document on the train, but apparently it's gone now.  So I'll just need to retype it.

To anyone who is thinking of staying in Taiwan for longer than 30 days.

I had planned to take a 98 day trip to Taiwan and spend it there without a visa, as the process to get the visa seemed somewhat daunting.  I had also been excited about the idea of traveling to other places in Southeast Asia.  I decided early on that maybe it'd just be easier to just go in on a landing visa and jump out every thirty days and go somewhere new.  Sounds stupid now, but at the time I thought it was a great idea.  It would give me the chance to see the area, more of the world and would force me to do it.  Then I came to realize that cost is a pretty big issue for a recently graduated college student and with my current supply of funds running dry and my new source smaller than the last I decided this wasn't such a good idea in relation to the overall cost.  It's also somewhat shady.

Being here in New York before I leave for Taipei, I used this opportunity to put what I needed together and apply for a 60 day multiple entry visa.  I know what you might be thinking, why only 60 days, aren't you staying for 98 days?  True, but the good thing about this, is according to the Taipei Economic and Cultural Office, the visa can be extended twice another 60 days each time, for a total of 180 days.  Well beyond what I plan to do, but a much safer bet.  The good news about this whole thing as opposed to paying $140 for each time I come back into Taiwan and forcing myself to pay airfare each time out. I can now just go out of the country when I choose and don't have to make as much of a financial commitment.

I'm excited for Taiwan, but like every place it seems I go for the past five years, money is tight and making sure that I'm spending the least I can while still having the fullest experience.
